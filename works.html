Literary Works

Masterpieces of Islamic Historiography and Literature

Allama Shibli Nomani was a prolific writer who authored more than 25 major works spanning biographies, history, literary criticism, and poetry. His works are characterized by rigorous research, critical analysis, and elegant prose that revolutionized Islamic scholarship in the Indian subcontinent.

Biography

Sirat-un-Nabi

The monumental biography of Prophet Muhammad, considered his magnum opus. Left incomplete and later finished by his student Syed Suleman Nadvi. A masterpiece of Islamic historiography that set new standards for biographical writing.

8 Volumes Urdu
Wikipedia Info
Biography

Al-Farooq

Landmark biography of the second Caliph, Umar ibn al-Khattab. Set new standards for Islamic biographical writing with critical historical analysis and comprehensive coverage of his life and contributions to Islamic civilization.

2 Volumes Urdu
Wikipedia Info
Biography

Al-Mamoon

Comprehensive biography of the Abbasid Caliph Al-Ma'mun, exploring his contributions to Islamic civilization, science, and the translation movement during the Golden Age of Islam.

1 Volume Urdu

Biography

Al-Ghazali

Detailed study of the life and works of Imam Al-Ghazali, focusing on his spiritual journey, philosophical contributions, and lasting impact on Islamic thought and Sufism.
